\section{Introduction}

This document catalogs the cost and reward functions used in the Multi-Agent Racing Safe MetaDrive Environment (marl\_safe\_metadrive\_env.py). The environment extends the SafeMetaDriveEnv to support Multi-Agent Reinforcement Learning (MARL) between two cars in a racing scenario, incorporating race-based rewards, continuous simulation despite collisions, and support for reverse driving.

\section{Cost Functions}

The cost functions in the Multi-Agent Racing Safe MetaDrive Environment measure safety violations during driving. The general cost function is defined as:

\begin{align}
J(\pi) &= \mathbb{E}\left[\sum_{t=0}^T \gamma^t\,C(s_t,a_t)\right] \\
C(s_t,a_t) &= C_{\text{crash\_vehicle}}(s_t) + C_{\text{crash\_object}}(s_t) + C_{\text{out\_of\_road}}(s_t) + C_{\text{on\_yellow\_line}}(s_t) + C_{\text{on\_white\_line}}(s_t)
\end{align}

Each component of the cost function is defined as follows:

\subsection{Crash Vehicle Cost}

\begin{align}
C_{\text{crash\_vehicle}}(s_t) = 
\begin{cases}
c_{\text{crash\_vehicle}} & \text{if vehicle crashes with another vehicle} \\
0 & \text{otherwise}
\end{cases}
\end{align}

Where $c_{\text{crash\_vehicle}} = 10.0$ is the cost parameter for crashing with another vehicle.

\subsection{Crash Object Cost}

\begin{align}
C_{\text{crash\_object}}(s_t) = 
\begin{cases}
c_{\text{crash\_object}} & \text{if vehicle crashes with an object} \\
0 & \text{otherwise}
\end{cases}
\end{align}

Where $c_{\text{crash\_object}} = 5.0$ is the cost parameter for crashing with an object.

\subsection{Out of Road Cost}

\begin{align}
C_{\text{out\_of\_road}}(s_t) = 
\begin{cases}
c_{\text{out\_of\_road}} \cdot (1.0 + 0.2 \cdot n_{\text{off\_road}}) & \text{if vehicle is out of road} \\
0 & \text{otherwise}
\end{cases}
\end{align}

Where $c_{\text{out\_of\_road}} = 20.0$ is the base cost parameter for driving out of road, and $n_{\text{off\_road}}$ is the number of consecutive steps the vehicle has been off-road. This creates an exponentially increasing penalty to strongly incentivize returning to the road.

\subsection{On Yellow Line Cost (Wrong Side of Road)}

\begin{align}
C_{\text{on\_yellow\_line}}(s_t) = 
\begin{cases}
c_{\text{on\_yellow\_line}} \cdot (1.0 + 0.3 \cdot n_{\text{wrong\_side}}^{1.5}) & \text{if on yellow continuous line} \\
0.5 \cdot c_{\text{on\_yellow\_line}} \cdot (1.0 + 0.3 \cdot n_{\text{wrong\_side}}^{1.5}) & \text{if on yellow broken line} \\
0 & \text{otherwise}
\end{cases}
\end{align}

Where $c_{\text{on\_yellow\_line}} = 3.0$ is the cost parameter for driving on a yellow line (wrong side of road), and $n_{\text{wrong\_side}}$ is the number of consecutive steps the vehicle has been on the wrong side. The cost increases exponentially the longer the vehicle stays on the wrong side.

\subsection{On White Line Cost}

\begin{align}
C_{\text{on\_white\_line}}(s_t) = 
\begin{cases}
c_{\text{on\_white\_line}} & \text{if on white continuous line} \\
0.5 \cdot c_{\text{on\_white\_line}} & \text{if on white broken line} \\
0 & \text{otherwise}
\end{cases}
\end{align}

Where $c_{\text{on\_white\_line}} = 1.0$ is the cost parameter for driving on a white line.

\section{Reward Functions}

The reward functions in the Multi-Agent Racing Safe MetaDrive Environment incentivize racing behavior, progress toward the destination, and competitive performance. The general reward function is defined as:

\begin{align}
R(s_t, a_t) = R_{\text{racing}}(s_t, a_t) + R_{\text{checkpoint}}(s_t) + R_{\text{termination}}(s_t)
\end{align}

Each component of the reward function is defined as follows:

\subsection{Racing Rewards}

The racing rewards component includes bonuses for being in the lead and winning the race:

\begin{align}
R_{\text{racing}}(s_t, a_t) = 
\begin{cases}
r_{\text{leading}} + \min(m_{\text{lead}} \cdot 0.01, 0.1) & \text{if agent is leading} \\
0 & \text{otherwise}
\end{cases}
+ 
\begin{cases}
r_{\text{winning}} & \text{if agent has won the race} \\
0 & \text{otherwise}
\end{cases}
\end{align}

Where:
\begin{itemize}
\item $r_{\text{leading}} = 0.1$ is the base reward for being in the lead
\item $m_{\text{lead}}$ is the margin by which the agent is leading (distance ahead of second place)
\item $r_{\text{winning}} = 10.0$ is the reward for winning the race
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Checkpoint Rewards}

The checkpoint rewards component provides incentives for reaching checkpoints along the track:

\begin{align}
R_{\text{checkpoint}}(s_t) = \sum_{c \in C_t} \left( r_{\text{checkpoint}} + \mathbf{1}_{\text{first}}(c) \cdot r_{\text{first\_bonus}} \right)
\end{align}

Where:
\begin{itemize}
\item $C_t$ is the set of checkpoints reached at time step $t$
\item $r_{\text{checkpoint}} = 2.0$ is the base reward for reaching a checkpoint
\item $\mathbf{1}_{\text{first}}(c)$ is an indicator function that equals 1 if the agent is the first to reach checkpoint $c$, and 0 otherwise
\item $r_{\text{first\_bonus}} = 1.0$ is the additional bonus for being the first to reach a checkpoint
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Termination Rewards}

The termination rewards component provides sparse rewards at the end of an episode:

\begin{align}
R_{\text{termination}}(s_t) = 
\begin{cases}
r_{\text{success}} & \text{if agent reaches destination} \\
-c_{\text{out\_of\_road}} & \text{if agent drives out of road} \\
-c_{\text{crash\_vehicle}} & \text{if agent crashes with vehicle} \\
-c_{\text{crash\_object}} & \text{if agent crashes with object} \\
0 & \text{otherwise}
\end{cases}
\end{align}

Where:
\begin{itemize}
\item $r_{\text{success}} = 10.0$ is the reward for successfully reaching the destination
\item $c_{\text{out\_of\_road}} = 20.0$ is the penalty for driving out of road
\item $c_{\text{crash\_vehicle}} = 10.0$ is the penalty for crashing with another vehicle
\item $c_{\text{crash\_object}} = 5.0$ is the penalty for crashing with an object
\end{itemize}

\section{Comparison with Standard MetaDrive Reward Functions}

The Multi-Agent Racing Safe MetaDrive Environment extends the standard MetaDrive reward functions with racing-specific components:

\begin{enumerate}
\item \textbf{Leading Reward}: Provides a continuous incentive for being in the lead position
\item \textbf{Winning Reward}: Offers a large one-time reward for winning the race
\item \textbf{Checkpoint Rewards}: Encourages progress through the track with intermediate rewards
\item \textbf{First-to-Checkpoint Bonus}: Adds competitive pressure by rewarding the first agent to reach each checkpoint
\end{enumerate}

The cost functions are also enhanced with:

\begin{enumerate}
\item \textbf{Escalating Off-Road Penalties}: Costs increase the longer an agent remains off-road
\item \textbf{Wrong-Side Penalties}: Costs for driving on the wrong side of the road, with higher penalties for continuous lines
\item \textbf{Lane Line Penalties}: Separate costs for crossing white lane lines
\end{enumerate}

These modifications create a more competitive racing environment while maintaining safety constraints.
